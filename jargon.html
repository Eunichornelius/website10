Here are some definitions of legal terms:
  • Acquittal: A verdict of not guilty, resulting in the release of the defendant
  • Adjournment: A postponement of a case to a later date
  • Adjudication: The legal process of resolving a dispute
  • Advocate: A legal representative, such as a barrister or solicitor, who represents a party in court
  • Affidavit: A written statement of facts that is sworn to by the affiant under oath
  • Affirmation: A declaration made by a witness who cannot take an oath
  • Answer: A pleading filed by the defendant in a civil case to respond to the plaintiff's claims
  • Damages: Compensation paid to a successful plaintiff by the defendant in a civil case
  • Defendant: The person, company, or organization that is being sued by the plaintiff
  • Injunction: A court order that requires or prohibits a party from doing something
  • Rescission: The cancellation of a contract, usually due to fraud or a material breach
  • Declaratory Judgment: A court's determination of the legal obligations and rights of the parties involved in a situation
  • Deferred sentence: A criminal proceeding where the punishment is postponed to a later date
  • Alimony: Money paid by one spouse to another during or after a divorce or separation
  • Alternative dispute resolution: A method of settling a dispute without a formal trial, such as arbitration, mediation, or conciliation
